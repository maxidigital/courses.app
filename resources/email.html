I just wanted to quickly follow up regarding the medical questionnaire. For insurance purposes, all the questions need to be answered with "No." If there's a "Yes" response to any of them, we're required to ask for a medical evaluation signed by a doctor confirming you're fit to dive. It's a simple check that can be done easily anywhere, and many general practitioners are familiar with this kind of evaluation.

So you have two options:

  1. You can get a brief medical clearance from a doctor stating you're fit for freediving.
  2. Or, if you're confident there's no actual concern, you can fill out the questionnaire again, answering "No" to all the questions.

We're not medical professionals ourselves, so we rely on this process to meet our insurance requirements.

Let me know how you'd like to proceed — we're looking forward to diving with you soon!
